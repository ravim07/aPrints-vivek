Letter from Papillion-La Vista South High Adviser to Nebraska JEA Teachers

I am writing to encourage Nebraska schools to seriously consider transitioning to a magazine format through aPrintis, a company the two Papillion-La Vista high schools have been using for the last 8 years. When first approached with this idea, I thought it sounded "too good to be true", honestly. But, being a new advisor who didn't feel any particular attachment to the format Papillion South had used in the past, I gave it a shot.

The price ($850 for 1,000 copies of a 16-page full-color, glossy magazine) was similar to what we were paying for sloppy newsprint that most students didn't want to touch.

The effect was truly electrifying. The professional look made students take the publication more seriously -- readers and creators alike. These publications are true keepsakes, unlike ever-changing websites and newsprint that turns yellow and brittle within months if not carefully preserved. I'm not one for making sales pitches, but I love journalism, and I see this format as a viable competitor in our students' flashy, media-saturated worlds.

We LOVE aPrintis’s recent feature of doing fund raising through subscriptions, donations and advertising and foresee it being a big boost to our program. We are already bumping up our page count to 24 for the December issue and are considering a move to 32 pages next semester, which amazingly costs only a quarter for double the pages!.

I can send you a few copies of the magazines Papillion South published this year, just so you can get a feel for the quality and envision the possibilities for your own school. Something to think about as you plan for next year....

Anne Albracht
Journalism Teacher,Yearbook and Magazine Adviser
Adviser, Titan Legacy Magazine
Papillion-La Vista South High School
Papillion, NE

Northview High, Duluth, GA Adviser to fellow advisers following an NSPA Workshop

Thank you for attending my session this year on juggling a new staff. For the advisers that had questions about printing here is some info that might help you.

We switched over to aPrintis in 2014, and we couldn’t be happier. They are great to work with and they have allowed us to really grow as a publication. We do 32-pages full-color and we get 1,000 copies printed each issue. The cost is $1175 and that includes shipping to the school. We were able to get 1,000 issues of 32-pages full color for the same price we were doing 750 issues of 24-pages half color.

Their quality is unmatched and the staff really works hard to help publications make a quality product. They are by far the cheapest magazine printer I found. I shopped it around a bunch, and I can’t say enough good things about them.

We fund our magazine through ad sales and issue sales. It has enabled our staff to do more creative designs with the increased space and color. Switching to the news magazine was the best creative decision we could have made.

Chris Yarbrough, Ed. S.
